Bidirectional Cardiorenal Coupling Model

This simulator couples two physiological models via bidirectional message passing to study cardiorenal syndrome (CRS) — the clinical condition where heart and kidney failure mutually accelerate each other.

Heart Model: Time-Varying Elastance

The left ventricle is modeled using a time-varying elastance framework with an exponential end-diastolic pressure-volume relationship (EDPVR). The key HFpEF parameter is stiffness_scale, which shifts the EDPVR upward — producing elevated filling pressures (LVEDP) with preserved ejection fraction.

Kidney Model: Hallow et al. 2017

Based on Hallow et al. (CPT:PSP, 2017), this module includes glomerular hemodynamics with autoregulation via tubuloglomerular feedback (TGF) and the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS). Nephron loss is modeled via Kf_scale, affecting ultrafiltration capacity.

Message Passing Protocol

Heart → Kidney: MAP (perfusion pressure), CO (cardiac output), CVP (venous congestion). Scaled by coupling intensity α.

Kidney → Heart: V_blood (volume from Na/water balance), SVR (RAAS vasoconstriction + CKD-associated resistance increase).

Key Innovation

The coupling intensity parameter α allows quantitative comparison of coupled vs. uncoupled disease trajectories, revealing how much each organ's dysfunction accelerates the other's decline through specific physiological mechanisms.

End-Diastolic Pressure-Volume Relationship

LVEDP = stiffness × A_ed × (exp(β × (EDV - V0)) - 1)

Single-Nephron GFR

SNGFR = Kf_eff × (P_gc - P_Bow - π_avg)
GFR = 2 × N_nephrons × SNGFR × 10-6

RAAS Regulation

RAAS_factor = clip(1.0 - RAAS_gain × 0.005 × (MAP - MAP_setpoint), 0.5, 2.0)

TGF Loop

MD_Na = GFR × C_Na × (1 - η_PT) × (1 - η_LoH)
TGF_err = (MD_Na - TGF_setpoint) / TGF_setpoint
R_AA = R_AA0 × (1 + TGF_gain × TGF_err)

Volume Balance

V_blood += (water_intake - water_excretion) × dt × 0.33
Na_total += (Na_intake - Na_excretion) × dt

Coupling Scaling

signal_coupled = signal_base + α × (signal_actual - signal_base)
